Juan A. Fraire
International Coordinator & South-American Partner B
Juan A. Fraire received the Telecommunications Engineering degree with important academic achievements and honors. Motorola's best-student scholarship and award unlocked his rapid involvement in mobile communication industries as a Motorola and Nokia Siemens Network engineer. With 5 years as a professional engineer, Juan built a strong background in developing, deploying and managing a wide range of 3G and 4G mobile networks. On-site support and training was also provided to customers around the world. Juan's expertise was further enhanced his PhD. in Engineering and Applied Sciences from the National University of Córdoba (UNC) in cooperation with the Argentinian Space Agency (CONAE). His main focus of research was the implementation and optimization of communication algorithms and protocols for mobile networks in space. With only 5 years as a researcher, Juan authored a book based on his contributions which have been recognized by colleagues from NASA, ESA, CNSA and more than 35 international journals and conferences. Juan also serves as an assistant professor in Computing Sciences in FAMAF university and since 2014, he is the chair of the annual Space-Terrestrial Internetworking (STINT) workshop. After completing a postdoctoral stage at a CNRS laboratory in France, Juan was awarded a researcher position at CONICET in Argentina from which he serves as a consultant in space networking for CONAE and in cloud computing technologies fro Cloud&Heat.
Sandra Céspedes
Chilean Coordinator
SANDRA CÉSPEDES received her B.Eng.(2003) and Specialization (2007) degrees in Telematics Engineering, and Management of Information Systems, from Universidad Icesi, Colombia, and a Ph.D. (2012) in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the University of Waterloo, Canada. She is currently an Associate Professor with the Department of Electrical Engineering and the head of the Wireless Networking Research Group (WiNet) at Universidad de Chile, Santiago, Chile. She is the Head of Research at NIC Chile Research Labs and an Associate Researcher with the Advance Center of Electrical and Electronic Engineering (AC3E). From 2012 to 2014, Dr. Céspedes was an Assistant Professor with the Department of Information and Communication Technologies, at Universidad Icesi in Cali, Colombia and currently holds an honorary Adjunct Professorship at the same University. Dr. Céspedes was elevated to IEEE Senior member since 2017 and was the recipient of the Best Undergraduate Teaching award at Universidad de Chile in 2019. Her research focuses on the topics of routing and protocols design for restricted devices and the Internet of Things, vehicular communications systems and networking, and cyberphysical systems. She serves as an Associate Editor for the IEEE Internet of Things Journal and the IEEE Vehicular Technology Magazine.
Pascal Berthou
French Partner A
Pascal Berthou (pascalberthcuelaasfr) received a Ph.D. in computer science and telecommunication from the National Polytechnic Institute of Toulouse in 2001. He is an associate professor at the University Paul Sabatier. He joined the Laboratory for Analysis and Architecture of Systems of the French National Centre for Scientific Research (LAAS-CNRS) in 1998 as a research staff member, where he works in the area of high-speed networks and protocols and multimedia communications. Since then he has covered two major areas of activity. The first area deals with satellite communication systems. The second research area is sensor networks, particularly communication systems and their application, which in recent years has been directed toward the design of WSN for instrumentation networks. Within this domain he has focused on hardware/software network interface issues and cross layering interactions to reduce the energy consumption in WSN. He recently started research contributions in the area of SON.
Nicola Accettura
French Partner B
Nicola Accettura received the Dottorato di Ricerca (Ph.D.) degree in information and communications technologies from the Scuola Interpolitecnica di Dottorato and the Politecnico di Bari, Italy, in 2013. He holds a post-doctoral position with the University of California at Berkeley, Berkeley, CA, USA. His main research activity is focused on designing a standards-based communication protocol stack for the Internet of Things (IoT). Within the developing team of the OpenWSN project, he is contributing in modeling scalable IoT networks. He is an active Member of the IETF 6TiSCH WG, which is defining protocols that make the IEEE802.15.4e TSCH perfectly interfaced with well-known IoT IETF standards, such as 6LoWPAN, RPL, and CoAP, thus enabling ultralow power and highly reliable mesh networks which are fully integrated into the Internet. Among the Internet-Drafts proposed by the IETF 6TiSCH WG, he has co-authored the draft related to the On-the-fly scheduling technique. He also serves as a Reviewer for many journals and conferences.
César Azurdía
Senior Researcher
Cesar A. Azurdia Meza received a bachelor's degree in electrical engineering from the Universidad del Valle de Guatemala in 2005, and a Master's degree in electrical engineering from the Linnaeus University, Sweden, in 2009. In 2013 he obtained a Ph. D. in Electronics and Radio Engineering at Kyung Hee University, Republic of South Korea. He joined the Department of Electrical Engineering at the University of Chile as an assistant professor in August 2013. He has served as a member of the Technical Program Committee (TPC) for multiple conferences, as well as a reviewer in journals such as IEEE Communications Letter, IEEE Transactions on Wireless Communications, Wireless Personal Communications and EURASIP Magazine on advances in signal processing. Dr. Azurdia is a member of the IEEE Communication Society, as well as a member of IEICE. His research interests include topics such as the Nyquist ISI criteria, OFDM-based systems, SC-FDMA, visible light communication systems, in-vehicle communications, 5G enablement technologies and enabling technologies, as well as technical signal processing for communication systems.
Claudio Estévez
Senior Researcher
Claudio Estevez received his Ph.D. degree in electrical and computer engineering and with a minor in computer science from the Georgia Institute of Technology, Atlanta, GA, USA, 2010; his M.S. degree in electrical and computer engineering with a minor in optical engineering from the University of Alabama in Huntsville, Huntsville, AL, USA, 2003; and his B. S. degree in electrical and computer engineering from the University of Puerto Rico, Mayaguez, PR, USA, 2001. In 2011, he became an assistant professor at the electrical engineering department at Universidad de Chile. Between 2012 and the present time Prof. Estevez has participated in several national and international research projects funded by the following grants: FONDECYT, FONDEF, CORFO, FONDEQUIP, and ERANet-LAC to study various topics such as: MAC protocols in WPAN using 60 GHz; remote polysomnographic monitoring; microgrid communication systems, adverse event monitoring of Chilean coasts, vehicular communication support systems, and other. His research interests include: Connection-oriented transport protocols, network fairness study, MAC-layer protocols in WPAN, 60-GHz WPAN applications, cloud computing with data mining/warehousing, wireless body area networks, remote healthcare, microgrid communication infrastructure, transport decision support systems.
